What is Carbon Credit?

A carbon credit is a permit that allows an entity (such as a company or country) to emit a certain amount of greenhouse gases, typically one metric ton of carbon dioxide (CO₂) or its equivalent. It is part of a system designed to limit, reduce, and offset carbon emissions to combat climate change. Organizations can buy, sell, or trade these credits in carbon markets.

How Carbon Credits Work

Carbon credits operate within a cap-and-trade system or voluntary carbon markets. Here's how they function:

1. Setting an Emission Limit (Cap)

Governments or regulatory bodies set a limit on how much CO₂ a company, industry, or country can emit. This is known as the carbon cap.

2. Allocating or Purchasing Carbon Credits

If a company emits less CO₂ than its allocated limit, it earns extra carbon credits. If a company emits more than its limit, it must buy carbon credits from others to offset its excess emissions.

3. Trading Carbon Credits

Companies that have unused carbon credits can sell them to companies that need more credits. This creates a carbon market where organizations trade credits based on supply and demand.

4. Carbon Offsetting

Companies or individuals can also purchase carbon credits to fund environmental projects that reduce CO₂, such as:

  • Reforestation (planting trees)
  • Renewable energy projects (solar, wind)
  • Methane capture projects (from landfills or agriculture)

By investing in these projects, emissions are offset, balancing out the carbon footprint.

Example of Carbon Credit Usage

A company is allowed to emit 1,000 tons of CO₂ per year but ends up emitting 1,200 tons. It can:

  • Buy 200 carbon credits from another company that emitted less than its cap.
  • Invest in a carbon offset project that removes 200 tons of CO₂ from the atmosphere.

Benefits of Carbon Credits

  • ✔ Encourages companies to reduce emissions
  • ✔ Promotes investment in green energy and sustainability projects
  • ✔ Provides a financial incentive for businesses to be more eco-friendly
  • ✔ Helps countries meet international climate targets like the Paris Agreement
